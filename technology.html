Computer Technology

The term “information technology” gets thrown around a lot these days, but what does it actually mean and why is it important? Computer information technology, or IT, is a relatively new field that first emerged in the 1990s when computers became the basic tools used for work and everyday life. With the rise in computer use came the need for professionals that could develop and maintain their infrastructure. Computer information technology is often used to refer to technical support, but it’s become much more than that. IT is the development, maintenance, and use of computer systems and networks; but it encompasses a wide variety of tech-based disciplines that deal with information and data. Security and communication are huge elements of the technology industry, especially as systems become more public and technology continues to evolve.

Why Is Information Technology Important?

Technology impacts nearly every aspect of our lives, including the way we communicate, learn, socialize, work, bank, and more. IT ensures that this technology—and the networks and software that connect it—operates effectively. Thanks to IT, organizations and individuals enjoy better connections through faster, more accessible communication and increased productivity through easier access to information. From finance to farming, nearly every industry relies on information technology, and this dependence is only going to become stronger.

What Areas Does Information Technology Include?

IT includes many career paths and disciplines, but the following are the five main professional areas of IT:

  1. Administrationhandles the daily operations and deployment of software upgrades, security, and data management.
  2. Support answers questions and troubleshoots problems.
  3. Applications works with third-party applications to deliver critical business capabilities and services.
  4. Compliance secures and monitors access to business data and applications to ensure they’re used according to business governance policies that meet regulatory requirements
  5. Cybersecurity develops and utilizes software that secures computer-based systems and keeps digital assets safe.
